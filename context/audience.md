# Audience

## Current priority audience

U.S. college students, primarily within the broader 18–34 market, who use an iPhone when preparing for work that requires concentration.

College students are the first audience to persuade. Job seekers and working professionals are not current priorities.

## Current situation

The student has already decided to study or complete concentration-heavy work and uses an iPhone as part of preparing the session.

The exact first content scenario has not yet been narrowed to a particular task such as reading, writing, problem-solving, or exam preparation.

## Problem moment

After deciding to focus but before beginning the intended work, the student chooses or adjusts timer, Screen Time, allowed-app, sound, break, or related settings.

## Current behavior and alternatives

Current alternatives may include:

- configuring a separate focus timer;
- opening Screen Time settings;
- choosing a sound in another app;
- revisiting break or cycle settings;
- moving through the Home Screen or app lists between those tools.

These behaviors are plausible based on the product problem definition and desk research. Their frequency among env users has not been established.

## Desired immediate result

Move from an existing decision to study into the intended focus session without rebuilding the recurring setup at that moment.

## Possible resistance

The student may worry that:

- env will become one more app to configure;
- a saved setup will be too rigid for different kinds of work;
- opening the same tools manually is already easy enough.

These are currently hypotheses, not confirmed env-user objections.

## Unverified audience assumptions

- College students commonly use the specific combination of timer, Screen Time, sound, and break settings described here.
- Setup choices are experienced as a concentration cost rather than harmless preparation.
- The student notices or values the difference between configuring tools and beginning the work.
- The internet-derived situations represent the current U.S. college audience accurately.
