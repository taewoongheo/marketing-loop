# Voice

## Language and region

- Visible marketing copy: English (U.S.).
- Internal planning and collaboration may be in Korean.
- Product name: `env`, written in lowercase. Mention it only when the product connection helps the content.

## Desired tone

- Quiet and private rather than performative.
- Minimal and emotionally specific.
- Gentle but not motivational.
- Direct but not harsh.
- Systemized without sounding clinical or technical.
- Natural for U.S. college students and adult learners.

## Sentence density

- Prefer one clear idea per sentence.
- Prefer concrete actions and objects over internal strategy labels.
- Keep the reader close to a recognizable starting moment.
- Remove explanation that does not change the reader's understanding.

## Reader distance

Speak to the reader as someone who already wants to do the work. Do not lecture them about discipline, blame them for phone use, or imply that they lack ambition.

## Prefer

- Concrete nouns such as timer, Screen Time, blocked apps, allowed apps, sound, break, first page, first problem, or first paragraph.
- Observable transitions between deciding to study, configuring the environment, and beginning the work.
- Limited claims about what env changes at the starting moment.

## Avoid

- Hustle, grind, discipline, or motivational language.
- Cyber, hacker, gamer, or “dopamine war” framing.
- Clinical explanations or ADHD claims.
- Guilt, scolding, and moral judgments about phone use.
- Generic productivity promises.
- Transformation claims and guaranteed outcomes.
- “All-in-one focus app” positioning.
- Feature-list language that reduces env to a collection of timer, blocking, and sound tools.
- Internal labels such as cognitive bottleneck, reach bottleneck, setup-choice fatigue, tool switching, mechanism, or belief shift in visible copy.

## Product mention

- Use `env`, not `Env`, unless it begins a sentence where lowercase remains visually acceptable.
- The product should appear as the prepared environment that supports the transition into work.
- Do not make env the hero of every slide or force the product into the hook.
