# Message MSG-001: The setup is not the work

**Status:** testing

## Target user and situation

A college student has already decided to do concentration-heavy work but is still choosing timer, Screen Time, allowed-app, sound, break, or cycle settings before beginning.

## Current belief

Choosing the focus setup is harmless preparation. If starting still feels difficult afterward, the problem must be a lack of discipline or focus.

## Current behavior

The student chooses or rebuilds several parts of the focus setup at the moment of starting while the intended work remains untouched.

## Intended belief shift

The setup is not the work. Repeating familiar setup choices can use attention that the student intended to bring to the first real study action.

This is the message to test, not an established scientific claim.

## Core perspective

Decide the recurring environment before the study session. At the moment of starting, run the prepared environment instead of choosing its parts again.

## Persuasion mechanism

1. Show the concrete choices still being made before work begins.
2. Separate “preparing to focus” from touching the intended work.
3. Show those values already decided and reusable.
4. End at the transition into the work, not at configuration as an achievement.

## Why env fits

env stores timer, app-blocking, allowed-app, sound, break, cycle, and related settings as one reusable environment and runs that prepared setup at the starting moment.

## Available evidence

- Verified product capability: the setup values can be saved and reused.
- Desk-research signal: students describe intending to study while continuing to use their phones.
- No direct env-user evidence currently proves that setup choices deplete concentration.

## Do not exaggerate

- Do not say each choice measurably lowers concentration unless evidence is added.
- Do not guarantee that env creates focus, prevents every distraction, or improves grades.
- Do not claim that env solves motivation or chooses the work.
- Do not present desk-research quotes as env-user testimony.
