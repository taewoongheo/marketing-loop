# Message MSG-002 v1: Focus setup breaks across too many tools

**Status:** testing
**Version:** 1

## Target user and situation

A college student has decided to study, then moves between a timer, Screen Time, a sound app, and the Home Screen before the real work begins.

## Current belief

Moving through several focus tools is simply the normal route into a productive study session. Opening each tool only takes a moment, so the route itself does not matter.

## Current behavior

The student opens and configures separate focus tools while repeatedly returning to app lists, the Home Screen, or other surfaces where unrelated apps remain available.

## Intended belief shift

Every transition between setup tools creates another opening to leave the work the student already intended to begin.

The frequency and impact of this behavior are message hypotheses, not established env-user facts.

## Core perspective

The useful phone tools do not need to disappear. Prepare their recurring settings as one environment so the study session can begin without taking the long route through separate setup tools.

## Persuasion mechanism

1. Follow the literal route from timer to Screen Time to sound app to Home Screen.
2. Show the intended page, paragraph, or problem still untouched during those transitions.
3. Introduce one unrelated app opening as a plausible interruption, not an inevitable outcome.
4. Contrast that route with running one prepared environment.
5. End on the intended study action, not on env itself.

## Reader resistance to address

- “env might become one more app to configure.” Show that it is prepared once and reused.
- “Opening the same tools myself is already easy.” Contrast the number of transitions and off-task openings, not only setup time.
- “Combining the setup might remove tools I need.” Show that necessary study apps can remain allowed.
- “One setup cannot fit every session.” Show different environments retaining different settings.

## Concrete scenes and language

Prefer observable scenes such as:

- setting a timer and then leaving to configure Screen Time;
- returning to the Home Screen between the timer and sound app;
- opening a feed while moving from app blocking to focus sound;
- finishing the setup while the first reading page still waits.

Prefer concrete terms such as `focus timer`, `Screen Time`, `sound app`, `Home Screen`, `feed`, `first page`, and `first problem`.

Translate internal strategy language into visible behavior. Do not use `tool switching`, `entry route`, `phone path`, or `one tap fixes distraction` as reader-facing explanations.

## Why env fits

env runs prepared timer, Screen Time, allowed-app, sound, break, and related settings together as one reusable focus environment. Widgets can optionally shorten entry into that environment but are not the core mechanism.

## Available evidence

- Verified product capability: the settings can be prepared and run together while necessary apps remain available.
- Desk-research signal: students describe intending to study but entering unrelated phone use near the beginning.
- No direct env-user evidence currently establishes how often transitions between focus tools cause off-task app use.

## Do not exaggerate

- Do not say every setup transition causes distraction.
- Do not claim that the phone itself must disappear.
- Do not guarantee focus or complete distraction prevention.
- Do not claim env replaces Screen Time.
