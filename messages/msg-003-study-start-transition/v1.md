# Message MSG-003 v1: Intending to study is not yet working

**Status:** testing
**Version:** 1

## Target user and situation

A college student has chosen what to study but has not yet read, written, answered, marked, or solved any part of it.

## Current belief

The student needs to feel fully focused and finish preparing before the study session can really begin.

## Current behavior

The student remains busy around the assignment or reading while no part of the chosen work changes.

## Intended belief shift

Intending, preparing, and staying near the work are not the same as beginning it. The student can make contact with a small part of the chosen work before feeling fully ready.

The idea that engagement may follow contact with the work is an unverified message hypothesis, not a product or scientific claim.

## Core perspective

Make the immediate transition more workable: keep the recurring study environment prepared, reduce the immediacy of competing apps, and define beginning by a visible action inside the chosen task.

## Persuasion mechanism

1. Show a student who knows what to study but is still active around it.
2. Contrast preparation with a concrete change in the assignment, reading, or problem.
3. Keep the first action small and specific without presenting it as a guaranteed productivity method.
4. Position env as support for the repeatable environment portion of the transition.
5. End with work touched or resumed, not with a completed setup ritual.

## Reader resistance to address

- “Another routine could become another way to postpone studying.” Keep every supporting step subordinate to visible contact with the chosen work.
- “Can beginning differently matter if I still do not feel focused?” Limit the comparison to remaining near the work versus completing a small part of it.
- “I am close enough to the assignment that the session has basically started.” Distinguish activity around the task from changing something inside it.

## Concrete scenes and language

Prefer observable scenes such as:

- remaining busy around an assignment without writing or answering anything;
- waiting to feel focused before reading the first paragraph;
- spending the beginning near a problem set without attempting the first problem;
- returning to one visible point left unfinished.

Prefer concrete terms such as `assignment`, `reading`, `paragraph`, `problem`, `answer`, and `unfinished line`.

Do not use internal labels such as `start friction`, `task action`, `entry route`, or `setup loop` in visible copy. Do not promise `instant focus` or `perfect focus`.

## Why env fits

env can prepare and run the repeatable timer, app-access, sound, break, and cycle settings around the transition. It does not choose the assignment, define the first task action, create motivation, control where the phone is placed, or perform the work.

## Available evidence

- Verified product capability: env can run a prepared study environment and reduce access to distracting apps while necessary apps remain allowed.
- Desk-research signal: students describe intending to study while continuing to use their phones near the start.
- No direct env-user evidence currently proves that beginning with a small task action creates engagement or improves study outcomes.

## Do not exaggerate

- Do not claim env chooses priorities or understands the work.
- Do not claim a small first action solves motivation, health, or performance problems.
- Do not claim env guarantees that the student starts immediately or remains focused.
- Keep env subordinate to the work itself.
