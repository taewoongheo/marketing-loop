# Message `msg-training-judgment-made-simple` v1: Evidence-informed judgment, simple execution

## Target situation

Within the primary audience defined in `context/product.md`, this message applies when a lifter is choosing a Program for his goal, schedule, experience, and equipment, or when recorded performance must become the next Weight and Reps.

## Problem pattern

The lifter wants a useful Program or next action without repeatedly researching training methods, comparing routines, or managing every progression decision himself. A generic or automated answer can be difficult to trust when its evidence, relevance to his situation, and connection to actual performance are unclear. Verifying it for himself recreates the complicated, time-consuming, and tedious work he wanted to avoid. The central tension is blind simplicity versus self-managed complexity.

## Belief shift

### From

I want a simple Program or next action, but I cannot tell whether a generic or automated answer fits me or deserves trust. To verify it, I have to study and manage the whole training system myself, which is complicated, time-consuming, and tedious.

### To

A Program or next action can remain simple without demanding blind trust when its evidence, relevant conditions, and connection to actual performance are clear enough to judge.

## Persuasion logic

### Message pillars

The pillars define the durable perceptions this message seeks to accumulate. A piece of content may express one or several pillars; no individual post must state the complete message.

#### Evidence-informed judgment

Training judgment should feel defensible because it rests on more than an unsupported `science-based` label.

#### Situation-fit Program and progression

A useful Program or next action depends on relevant conditions such as goal, experience, schedule, available time, equipment, priorities, and actual performance rather than one universal answer.

#### Simple execution

Depth of training judgment and low execution burden can coexist; the lifter should not have to reconstruct the underlying system before using the decision.

### Persuasion routes

The routes define distinct reasons a piece of content or product experience may make the belief shift credible. Several routes may work together.

#### Problem recognition

Make the tension between opaque simplicity and tedious self-management recognizable so the audience sees the need for a third option.

#### Immediate payoff

Let the audience experience value before accepting broader authority by providing a bounded routine, decision, or action that can be used immediately.

#### Substantiation

Make a training decision credible by making its relevant supporting evidence visible.

#### Causal intelligibility

Make a training decision understandable by showing how its relevant basis leads to the practical conclusion.

#### Self-relevance

Show that changing a relevant condition can justify a different routine or next action, demonstrating relevance without claiming universal personalization.

#### Discriminative contrast

Clarify the replacement belief by comparing it with an opaque, generic, random, or unnecessarily complex alternative without manufacturing a false binary.

#### Decisional relief

Demonstrate delegated judgment by reducing many possible exercises or methods to the few choices relevant to a defined situation.

#### Direct experience

Make the belief shift tangible through direct use of the planned product experience defined in `context/product.md` rather than relying on content claims alone.

## Resistance and response

| Resistance | Response |
| --- | --- |
| “Scientific” is just fitness marketing. | Credibility comes from showing the specific basis for a decision rather than relying on authority language. |
| This routine cannot fit everyone. | It does not have to; its relevance is bounded by the goal, schedule, equipment, experience, or other condition it addresses. |
| Situation-fit advice sounds complicated. | The conditions can remain precise while their practical consequence becomes a clear routine, alternative, or next action. |
| I already have a Program. | The message also applies to turning actual performance within an existing Program into the next progression decision. |
| Simple means shallow. | Let simplicity describe what the viewer has to do, not the depth of the judgment used to produce it. |

## Product role

LIFT CODE is the planned product embodiment of the belief shift: within the capabilities and boundaries defined in `context/product.md`, it takes over recurring training judgment while preserving justified rather than blind simplicity.

## Evidence and limits

### Supported

- Product direction, mechanisms, reference-app composition, and boundaries: `context/product.md`.
- Public competitor-user language expresses demand for complete Workouts without figuring everything out alone, history-based Weight recommendations, existing-Program progression, and reduced decision burden: `context/user-language.md`.
- The original Fitbod, Alpha Progression, and Hevy reference analyses document recurring review complaints about generated Programs or recommendations conflicting with stated goals and conditions, requiring repeated manual correction, or providing too little visible reason to judge the recommendation. This supports the existence of a recommendation-fit and trust problem in those reviewed source sets; it does not establish that every app or user has the problem. Internal provenance: Obsidian `레퍼런스 분석/Fitbod.md`, `레퍼런스 분석/Alpha Progression.md`, and `레퍼런스 분석/Hevy.md`.

### Message hypotheses

- The audience experiences a meaningful tension between wanting simple training decisions and wanting enough basis to trust those decisions.
- Visible evidence, relevant conditions, and connection to actual performance can reduce the need to choose between blind acceptance and self-managed complexity.
- Repeated useful content expressing these pillars will build a coherent account perception even when individual posts do not state the full message.
- Trust earned through useful content will transfer to interest in LIFT CODE.
- The audience will value delegated Program and progression decisions without interpreting the product as generic, shallow, or unnecessarily complex.

### Message-specific limits

- The available evidence does not establish how common the trust problem is across the broader target audience.
- The message does not establish recommendation accuracy, fit in every case, user outcomes, or future conversion.
- Scientific evidence may support a training principle used by the product or content; it does not make the planned recommendation system itself scientifically proven, validated, optimal, or superior.
