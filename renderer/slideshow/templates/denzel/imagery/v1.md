# Imagery: denzel v1

## 1. Scope and ownership

This file owns how approved slide meaning is expressed through generated or fixed imagery in the `denzel` slideshow: visual tone, image–copy coordination, slide-role adaptation, cross-slide consistency, prompt construction, generation settings, and visual exclusions.

- The selected message version owns the persuasion strategy and claim limits.
- The selected copywriting version owns the actual hook, body, CTA, title, caption, and all language rules.
- `materials.md` owns approved method inputs, not image prompts.
- `template.json` owns the live slide count and order, image-slot presence, geometry, crop, and fixed product image.
- The editable project owns the exact approved copy and resulting image bytes used for one content.
- `references/` provides structural and visual evidence only. Never use a reference screenshot as a production asset or reproduce its distinctive subject.

Do not store post-specific copy, a duplicate slide count, final prompts, or final images in this file. At generation time, read the approved project copy and live image slots together with this version. Dependency on those inputs does not transfer their ownership to imagery.

## 2. Image–copy interface

For each live image slot that permits source editing, derive one visual scene from the approved meaning of that slide.

- Let the copy make the argument. The image supplies recognizable situation, mood, visual texture, or one human-scale action.
- Extract the reader-facing situation, consequence, or action rather than converting the slide sentence word for word.
- Show one dominant idea per image. Do not combine every sentence, object, or method from the slide into one scene.
- Do not place the slide copy inside the image or rely on generated text to communicate the point.
- Avoid narrating through the prompt what the fixed product image or other visible UI already establishes.
- Keep the image truthful to the approved copy. Do not invent grades, money, intelligence, deadlines, distress, or other unsupported stakes to make the scene more dramatic.

### Semantic slide roles

Use the role found in the live project; do not assume a fixed number of generated slides.

- **Hook:** visualize the recognizable situation, friction, or consequence that makes the topic personally relevant. Favor the lived moment over an abstract mechanism.
- **Method:** visualize one concrete action or ordinary situation that supports the method. The image may be literal, lightly metaphorical, or atmospheric, but must not introduce a second method.
- **CTA:** preserve the template’s fixed product image when its source is not editable. Do not generate a replacement merely to make every slide visually uniform.

## 3. Visual tone

- Use believable, casual point-and-shoot photography with natural available light or a subtle direct-flash feel.
- Favor tactile, slightly grainy, lived-in scenes over polished productivity stock photography or glossy advertising.
- Keep rooms, desks, study materials, clothing, and gestures ordinary rather than aspirational or luxury-coded.
- Prefer candid framing, partial faces, hands, and natural body posture when they communicate the moment more credibly than a posed portrait.
- Allow modest imperfection and visual texture, but keep the focal action immediately legible after the template crop.
- Keep generated slides within one photographic family through compatible lighting, realism, grain, and color treatment. Do not force the same person or location unless the approved content requires continuity.
- Represent the target audience without turning ethnicity, gender, appearance, or campus identity into the point of the image.

## 4. Composition and prompt adaptation

- Read the live slot’s aspect ratio and crop behavior before choosing output dimensions; do not hardcode the template’s slot geometry here.
- Compose for the final crop, with the main subject and action inside a safe central region and without essential details at the edges.
- Use one plausible environment and only the props needed to make the action recognizable.
- Name the photographic treatment, subject, action, setting, composition, and exclusions in each prompt. Do not copy the approved slide wording into the prompt when a plain semantic description is clearer.
- For written study materials, request abstract or unreadable marks unless legible text is essential and separately approved.
- Keep phones face down, screens obscured, or interfaces unreadable unless a truthful visible interface is necessary to the slide.

## 5. Exclusions

Do not generate:

- readable captions, labels, logos, watermarks, or brand marks;
- fake `env` interfaces or unapproved product UI;
- polished corporate stock-photo posing, exaggerated studio lighting, or generic productivity-ad aesthetics;
- visual claims that exceed product truth or the selected message’s evidence limits;
- copied reference subjects, compositions, signatures, or distinctive expressions;
- collages, split screens, before/after layouts, or multiple competing scenes unless the live template and approved content explicitly require them;
- obvious anatomy defects, impossible objects, or malformed study materials.

## 6. Generation profile

For the current one-result-per-slide workflow:

- Provider: OpenAI Images API
- Model: `gpt-image-2`
- Quality: `medium`
- Results per eligible slot: exactly `1`
- Subjective selection: none
- Subjective regeneration: none
- Output format: PNG
- Output dimensions: choose one model-supported size that closely matches the live slot ratio
- Credentials: read `OPENAI_API_KEY` from the Git-ignored project `.env`; never store or print its value

Use the first successfully returned image directly. A request may be retried only when no image was returned because of a technical API or transport failure; do not issue another paid generation because the first image is merely less attractive than expected.

## 7. v1 rationale

The initial GPT Image 2 medium test produced one usable result for each editable Denzel image slot. v1 separates the reusable visual behavior discovered in that test from copywriting. It preserves the established casual, tactile image–copy relationship while making approved copy and the live template explicit runtime inputs rather than duplicating either one here.
